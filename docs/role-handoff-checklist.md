# OctoAcme Role Handoff Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist ensures smooth transitions when someone leaves a role or hands off responsibilities to another team member. Use this to maintain continuity and prevent knowledge loss.

---

## General Handoff Process (All Roles)

### Transition Timeline
- **2-4 weeks notice**: Ideal for comprehensive handoff
- **1-2 weeks**: Minimum for basic handoff with follow-up support

### Pre-Handoff Preparation
- [ ] Identify successor or interim coverage
- [ ] Schedule handoff meetings (multiple sessions if needed)
- [ ] Document current state and in-flight work
- [ ] Compile list of key contacts and escalation paths
- [ ] Organize and share all relevant artifacts and resources

### During Handoff
- [ ] Walk through current priorities and status
- [ ] Explain context and history of key decisions
- [ ] Shadow key meetings together for 1-2 cycles
- [ ] Introduce successor to key collaborators
- [ ] Transfer access and permissions
- [ ] Review common challenges and how to handle them

### Post-Handoff
- [ ] Be available for questions during transition period (1-2 weeks recommended)
- [ ] Schedule a follow-up check-in after 1-2 weeks
- [ ] Document any gaps discovered for future handoffs

**Current Work**
- [ ] List all assigned issues/PRs and their status

- [ ] Share context on current implementation approach

**Knowledge Transfer**

- [ ] Testing strategy and coverage expectations
- [ ] Deployment process and CI/CD pipeline details

**Key Contacts**

- [ ] Scrum Master (for process questions)

**Access & Tools**

## Emergency Handoff (Unexpected Departures)

When there's limited time for handoff:

### Priority 1 (Do Immediately)
- [ ] Document current critical work and deadlines
- [ ] Transfer any urgent decision-making authority
- [ ] Provide access to key systems and tools
- [ ] List critical contacts and escalation paths

### Priority 2 (Within 1-2 Days)
- [ ] Share location of all key artifacts
- [ ] Brief replacement on active issues/blockers
- [ ] Introduce replacement to immediate team

### Priority 3 (Within 1 Week)
- [ ] Make yourself available for async questions (email, chat)
- [ ] Provide any additional context as questions arise
- [ ] Schedule follow-up call if needed

## Handoff Quality Checklist

Use this to verify handoff completeness:

- [ ] Successor understands their key responsibilities
- [ ] Successor has access to all necessary tools and systems
- [ ] Successor knows who to contact for different types of questions
- [ ] In-flight work is clearly documented with status and next steps
- [ ] Key meetings and ceremonies have been explained
- [ ] Successor feels prepared to start the role (ask them!)
- [ ] Team has been notified of the transition
- [ ] Follow-up support plan is in place

## Tips for Effective Handoffs

### For Outgoing Team Member
- Start early - don't wait until the last minute
- Be thorough but prioritize critical information
- Write things down even if you think they're obvious
- Make yourself available for follow-up questions
- Focus on context and "why," not just "what"

### For Incoming Team Member
- Ask lots of questions
- Take notes during handoff sessions
- Request access to tools and systems early
- Shadow key meetings before taking over
- Follow up on anything unclear
- Be patient with yourself during the learning curve

### For Project/Team Leadership
- Build handoff time into transition plans
- Encourage documentation as an ongoing practice
- Support overlap period between outgoing and incoming
- Check in with new person after handoff
- Capture lessons learned to improve process
