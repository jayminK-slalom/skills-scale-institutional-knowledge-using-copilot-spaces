# OctoAcme Role Onboarding Guide

## Purpose
This guide helps new team members understand their role and get up to speed quickly on OctoAcme project processes, responsibilities, and key interactions.

---

## General Onboarding (All Roles)

### Week 1: Orientation
- [ ] Review the [Project Management Overview](octoacme-project-management-overview.md)
- [ ] Read your specific role definition in [Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)
- [ ] Review the Role Interaction Matrix to understand collaboration points
- [ ] Get access to project repositories, boards, and communication channels
- [ ] Attend team standup and introduce yourself
- [ ] Schedule 1:1s with key collaborators (see your role's Key Interactions section)

### Week 2-4: Integration
- [ ] Shadow key meetings and ceremonies relevant to your role
- [ ] Review recent project artifacts (status reports, retrospectives, release notes)
- [ ] Understand current project phase and priorities
- [ ] Begin taking on role-specific tasks with support from team

### Ongoing
- [ ] Participate in all role-specific ceremonies and activities
- [ ] Provide feedback on onboarding experience to improve this guide
- [ ] Build relationships with cross-functional collaborators

## Role-Specific Onboarding

### Developers
**Key Documents**: [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md), [Release & Deployment](octoacme-release-and-deployment.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Set up local development environment
- [ ] Review branching strategy and PR conventions
- [ ] Understand CI/CD pipeline and test requirements
- [ ] Review code standards and Definition of Done
- [ ] Pair with another developer on a small task

**Key Meetings**: Daily standup, sprint planning, sprint review, code reviews

**Primary Contacts**: Tech Lead, Scrum Master (blockers), Product Manager (requirements), UX Designer (design specs)

---

### Product Manager
**Key Documents**: [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md), [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Roles and Personas](octoacme-roles-and-personas.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Review product vision and success metrics
- [ ] Understand current roadmap and backlog priorities
- [ ] Meet with Sponsor to align on strategic objectives
- [ ] Meet with Business Analyst to understand requirements process
- [ ] Review customer feedback and usage metrics

**Key Meetings**: Weekly PM sync, sprint planning, sprint review, stakeholder updates

**Primary Contacts**: Project Manager, Sponsor, Business Analyst, UX Designer, Tech Lead

---

### Project Manager
**Key Documents**: All process documents, especially [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Risks & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Review project charter and timeline
- [ ] Understand current risks and dependencies
- [ ] Meet with Sponsor for strategic context
- [ ] Review communication plan and stakeholder landscape
- [ ] Understand team capacity and resource constraints

**Key Meetings**: All major ceremonies, weekly PM sync, stakeholder updates, risk reviews

**Primary Contacts**: All team members, Sponsor, Product Manager, Scrum Master

---

### Scrum Master
**Key Documents**: [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md), [Retrospective & Continuous Improvement](octoacme-retrospective-and-continuous-improvement.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Understand current sprint cadence and team norms
- [ ] Review team velocity and burndown metrics
- [ ] Identify current blockers and impediments
- [ ] Observe existing ceremonies to understand team dynamics
- [ ] Review retrospective action items and improvement history

**Key Meetings**: Daily standup (facilitate), sprint planning, sprint review, retrospectives

**Primary Contacts**: Developers, Project Manager, Product Manager

---

### Business Analyst
**Key Documents**: [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Review existing requirements documentation
- [ ] Understand key business processes and workflows
- [ ] Meet with primary stakeholders to introduce yourself
- [ ] Review backlog and upcoming work needing analysis
- [ ] Understand UAT process and testing approach

**Key Meetings**: Backlog refinement, requirements workshops, sprint planning, UAT sessions

**Primary Contacts**: Product Manager, stakeholders, Developers, Project Manager, UX Designer

---

### UX Designer
**Key Documents**: [Project Planning](octoacme-project-planning.md), [Execution & Tracking](octoacme-execution-and-tracking.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Review existing design system and style guide
- [ ] Understand user personas and research findings
- [ ] Review current and upcoming user-facing features
- [ ] Set up design tools and access to prototypes
- [ ] Meet with Product Manager to understand user experience goals

**Key Meetings**: Sprint planning, design reviews, sprint demos, usability testing sessions

**Primary Contacts**: Product Manager, Developers, Business Analyst

---

### Sponsor
**Key Documents**: [Project Initiation](octoacme-project-initiation.md), [Risks & Communication](octoacme-risks-and-communication.md)

**First Week Priorities**:
- [ ] Review project charter and business case
- [ ] Understand key success metrics and strategic objectives
- [ ] Meet with Project Manager and Product Manager for status
- [ ] Review high-level risks and resource needs
- [ ] Understand escalation needs and decision points

**Key Meetings**: Project kickoff, milestone reviews, executive steering committee, major decision points

**Primary Contacts**: Project Manager, Product Manager, key stakeholders

---

## Tips for Success

### For New Team Members
- Ask questions early and often
- Document what you learn to help the next person
- Build relationships proactively
- Understand the "why" behind processes, not just the "what"

### For Current Team Members
- Welcome new members and offer to answer questions
- Share context and history that may not be documented
- Be patient and encourage questions
- Pair with new members to accelerate learning
